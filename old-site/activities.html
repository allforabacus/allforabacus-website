    • Command line foo


    (21 January 2012, College Internet cafe)



    ABACUS organized a class on Shell Scripting intented mainly for s4 students.

    Ershad K, put together some topics, some of the very interesting things to do with shell scripting. His class mainly focused on the following:

    • Introduction to basic terminal commands.

    • Advantage of using VIM editor.

    • An idea of Cryptography which is used for hacking.

    • SSH (Secure Shell Host) for providing secure network services between two or more networked computers.

    • Demonstration of some operations performed by shell scripts like file manipulation, program execution, and printing text.

    • Things which can be performed using terminal, such as:
      - playing mp3 songs
      - accesing internet & performing google search
      - sending & reading mails
      - downloading web pages

    • ‘vinagre’ which is used to control networked computers using Remote Desktop technology

    • ‘scp’ which is a tool used to send files to networked computers.

    Workshop on Play! and beagleBone


    (20 December 2011, e-One hall)



    ABACUS organized a mini project guidance class for the S6 Computer Science students.

    The very first Play! and BeagleBone workshop in South India conducted by Pramod Sir.

    He did a brilliant demonstration and hands on session of the exciting new Play! framework. Starting from the very basics of setting it up to interfacing it with the BeagleBone, he went through the internal workings of the framework.


    Pramod sir also explained the idea of how the Internet working. This was an enlightening knowledge to the students as they finally figured out what was really happening behind the scene.

    At the end, after winding up the workshop, he discussed various project ideas with the students.

    .

    Beginner Workshop on Python + Embedded Systems


    (20 December 2011, Trichur)



    A workshop based on Python + Embedded systems conducted for Abacus members on 24th September, by Pramod Sir at his office near Government Engineering college Trichur.


    The workshop explored how to interact with the real world and conduct experiments using Python and an innovative open hardware device called the ExpEyes (which is part of the “Phoenix” project of the “Inter- University Accelerator Centre”, New Delhi).



    The workshop gave students a peep into the amazing possibilities offered by Free Software and Open Hardware. It was a beginner level workshop and 15 students were participated.

    Software Freedom Day Celebrations


    (17 September, 2011., College Internet cafe)



    ABACUS registered in the SFD site. This is the link: http://wiki.softwarefreedomday.org/2011/India/Kerala



    ABACUS celebrated the Software Freedom Day by conducting two workshops.


    Workshop 1


    A workshop on Installation/Familiarization of the GNU/Linux operating system for the students of all branches conducted by Anuraj sir of CSE Department. The workshop covered hard disk partitioning, installation of multiple operating systems side by side, use of Gparted (a superb partitioning tool), a quick idea of software installation and usage of GNU/Linux.


    Workshop 2


    A workshop on “How to start development on free software” conducted for the CS students by Ershad. It covered downloading of an open source project, installing it with the help of automake tools and finally modifying the source code. It was followed by an introductory class on git version control system and its use.

    Talk on Entrepreneurship


    (10 March 2011, College Auditorium)



    ABACUS conducted its first inaugural programme on 10th March 2011. It was aimed at providing the members with an idea of what all opportunities lay in the world outside which could be accomplished by one’s self.


    It was decided to conduct a talk on entrepreneurship. Shalin Jain, the CEO of tenmiles corporation was invited. A well, known entrepreneur in India, he started his own company at the age of 18 and made it a success over the years


    He shared his experiences with all of the ABACUS members gracefully. The speech was much motivational and he had a few points that all of us could take to life. “To follow what you love”, was his message. Concluding his talk in a span of 45 minutes, he threw an open session of Q & A, which was welcomed by the students.
